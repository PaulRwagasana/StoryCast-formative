Urban Echoes - Africa's Most Beautiful Cities

Africa as a continent is known for its diverse landscape, Rich culture and vibrant cities. Africa is home to some of the most beautiful cities in the world, attracting visitors from every corner of the world. In today's video we are covering the top 20 most beautiful cities in Africa.

Number 20: Gaborone, Botswana - Gaborone is the capital and the largest city in Botswana located in the Southeastern part of the country. The city has a population of 248,000 people.

Number 19: Maputo, Mozambique - Maputo is the capital and the largest city in Mozambique located along the Indian Ocean Coast. Maputo is a port city with an economy that is centered on Commerce. The city has a population of about 1.1 million people.

Number 18: Kampala, Uganda - Kampala serves as the capital and the largest city in Uganda with a population of about 2 million people. Kampala is reported to be one of the fastest growing cities in Africa.

Number 17: Abidjan, Ivory Coast - Abidjan is the economic capital and the largest city in Ivory Coast with a population of about 6.3 million people, making it the sixth largest city proper in Africa. The city is located on the east coast of the country on the Gulf of Guinea.

Number 16: Dar es Salaam, Tanzania - Dar es Salaam is the economic capital and the largest city in Tanzania with a population of about 8 million people, making it one of the largest cities in Africa. The city is located on the Indian Ocean coast and is an important economic Center.

Number 15: Luanda, Angola - Luanda is the capital and the largest city in Angola with a population of about 8 million people. Located along the Atlantic Ocean Coast, the city serves as the primary Port of Angola and a major industrial, cultural, and urban center.

Number 14: Windhoek, Namibia - Windhoek is the capital and the largest city in Namibia with a population of about 500,000 people. The city is located on the Khomas Highland plateau and is an important social, economic, political and Cultural Center in Namibia.

Number 13: Cotonou, Benin - Cotonou is the economic capital and the largest city of Benin with a population of about 1.5 million people. Located along the Gulf of Guinea, Cotonou serves as the seat of the government of Benin although Porto-Novo serves as the official Capital.

Number 12: Kigali, Rwanda - Kigali serves as the capital and the largest city of Rwanda with a population of about 1.8 million people. The city is located at the center of the country in a region of Rolling Hills with a series of valleys and ridges joined by steep slopes.

Number 11: Addis Ababa, Ethiopia - Addis Ababa which means "new flower" in the Amharic language is the official capital and the largest city in Ethiopia with a population of about 6.5 million people. Addis Ababa is the highest capital city in Africa, standing at an elevation of 2,355 meters above sea level.

Number 10: Dakar, Senegal - Dakar serves as the capital and the largest city of Senegal with a population of about 4 million people. The city is located in the Cap-Vert peninsula, the Western most point of Mainland Africa.

Number 9: Zanzibar City, Tanzania - The city of Zanzibar or Mjini District, often simply referred to as Zanzibar, is one of the two administrative districts of Mjini Magharibi region in Tanzania. The city is made up of two parts: Stone Town and Ng'ambo.

Number 8: Johannesburg, South Africa - Johannesburg, commonly known as Jo'burg or the "city of gold", is the largest city in South Africa with a population of about 5 million people. Johannesburg is one of the capital cities of South Africa and serves as the provincial capital of Gauteng, the wealthiest province in South Africa.

Number 7: Mombasa, Kenya - Mombasa is the second largest city in Kenya with a population of about 1.5 million people. The city is located along the Indian Ocean Coast in the Southeastern part of Kenya. The city served as the capital city of British East Africa before Nairobi was granted the capital status.

Number 6: Cairo, Egypt - Cairo, the capital and the largest city in Egypt, is one of the largest cities in Africa and the Middle East with the metro area having a population of about 21 million people. Cairo is a historic City and is one of the most visited cities in Africa due to its pyramids and the Sphinx.

Number 5: Lagos, Nigeria - Lagos is the economic capital and the largest city in Nigeria. Loosely classified into two main geographical areas: the island and the mainland, with a population of about 22 million people, Lagos is currently the largest city in Africa.

Number 4: Accra, Ghana - Accra is the capital and the largest city in Ghana with a population of about 3 million people. Located on the southern coast at the Gulf of Guinea, the name Accra is derived from the Akan word "Nkran" which means "ants" in reference to the many ant hills around the city.

Number 3: Nairobi, Kenya - Nairobi is the capital and the largest city in Kenya with a population of about 5 million people. Nairobi is the only capital city in the world with a National Park located within the city, and for this reason the city has been named the "Safari capital of Africa".

Number 2: Marrakech, Morocco - Marrakech is the fourth largest city in Morocco and is one of the four Imperial cities of Morocco. The city is located west of the foothills of the Atlas Mountains and is the capital of the Marrakech-Safi region.

Number 1: Cape Town, South Africa - And finally, topping the list of the top 20 most beautiful cities in Africa is Cape Town, South Africa. The city of Cape Town is nestled beneath the iconic Table Mountain and has a population of approximately 5 million people. With its laid-back atmosphere and friendly people, Cape Town is one of the best places to visit in the world.

Thank you for watching this video to the end. Be sure to like, share and subscribe as I bring you more information on the African continent, people and culture.
